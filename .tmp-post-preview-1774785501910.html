Nigeria Funding & Grants Briefing for Entrepreneurs

As we approach the end of the first quarter of 2026, the Nigerian entrepreneurial landscape is witnessing a surge in grant and loan opportunities. With the Small and Medium Enterprises Development Agency of the country (SMEDAN) planning to formalize one million businesses and provide a ₦12 billion funding boost, the time to act is now. In this briefing, we will outline the latest developments and provide actionable steps for founders to secure funding. Practical Moves for Founders To take advantage of the current funding landscape, entrepreneurs can take the following steps: Review the list of business grants available in the country and submit applications before the March 2026 deadline. This includes opportunities from SMEDAN and other government agencies. Explore the complete guide to getting a business loan in the country, which outlines the requirements and procedures for securing funding from various lenders. Prepare a solid business plan and financial projections to increase the chances of securing grants or loans. This includes developing a clear understanding of the business model, market, and financial requirements. Register and formalize the business to access funding opportunities and benefits from government agencies such as SMEDAN.
